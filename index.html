Executive MBA Perspective

My Executive MBA provided a structured framework for evaluating technology initiatives through the lenses of strategy, finance, organizational behavior, and market viability. Coursework and projects focused on strategic analysis, financial decision-making, product-market evaluation, leadership, and organizational effectiveness.

The experience strengthened my ability to connect technical execution with business outcomes, evaluate investment decisions, and communicate effectively with executive stakeholders.

One of the most valuable lessons was recognizing when an opportunity should not be pursued. Building Ealu.ai provided an opportunity to apply customer discovery, market analysis, and business-model evaluation in practice. The result was a deeper appreciation for disciplined decision-making and resource allocation.

Today I approach programs not only from a delivery perspective, but also through the lens of business value, strategic alignment, organizational impact, and long-term sustainability.

Product delivery

Ealu.ai

Applying Product, Governance, and Delivery Principles to AI

Ealu.ai serves as a practical environment for exploring how governance, compliance, product strategy, and delivery practices must evolve for AI-powered products.

The platform applies lessons learned from fintech, healthcare-adjacent systems, and enterprise release governance to modern AI workflows—treating security, privacy, trust, and operational readiness as first-class product requirements.

  • Two-factor authentication from initial release—not a later add-on
  • Privacy by design, including SSN detection and suppression in uploaded documents
  • Human-centered AI focused on clarity, trust, and reduced user friction

Flagship product: Intake Flow · Live walkthrough and GitHub source review available upon request.
