Crisis program management

Intuit Payroll

Delivering the Paycheck Protection Program in 18 Days

Context

In 2020, the CARES Act created the Paycheck Protection Program (PPP) to provide emergency funding for small businesses during the COVID-19 pandemic.

For Intuit's payroll customers, speed was critical. Businesses needed immediate access to PPP-related capabilities while regulatory guidance continued to evolve.

Challenge

The team had approximately 18 days from legislation to delivery.

Requirements were changing rapidly, multiple stakeholders were involved, and the solution needed to support compliance across federal, state, and local jurisdictions.

My Role

As Senior Technical Program Manager for Intuit Payroll, I coordinated delivery efforts across engineering, product, operations, and tax teams.

I managed dependencies, tracked delivery risks, coordinated stakeholder communication, and helped drive execution under an extremely compressed timeline.

Execution

  • Coordinated cross-functional teams to align on scope and delivery priorities.
  • Worked with Tax Operations teams to understand jurisdiction-specific requirements.
  • Managed delivery risks and dependencies throughout implementation.
  • Supported rapid post-launch response, including six hotfix releases over a two-week period.

Outcome

  • Delivered PPP capabilities within 18 days of the legislation becoming law.
  • Helped enable more than $10B in loans.
  • Supported more than 100,000 small-business jobs during a period of significant economic uncertainty.

Lessons Learned

High-velocity programs succeed when communication, risk management, and decision-making mechanisms are established before they are needed. Under extreme time pressure, execution discipline becomes even more important than process.

Release engineering

Fiserv

Modernizing Release Delivery at Scale

Context

As online banking and payments platforms grew in scale and complexity, release processes became increasingly difficult to manage. Deployment windows required approximately two hours, release activities were heavily manual, and audit requirements continued to increase.

Challenge

The organization needed to improve deployment speed while maintaining the controls, auditability, and operational discipline required in a regulated financial environment.

Reducing release duration alone was not enough. The solution needed to improve predictability, strengthen governance, and reduce operational risk across multiple teams and products.

My Role

As Director of Release Engineering, I built and led a globally distributed release engineering organization responsible for deployment automation, release governance, audit readiness, and production release execution.

Execution

  • Built and scaled a release engineering organization spanning multiple countries and time zones.
  • Standardized release processes, approval workflows, and production readiness criteria.
  • Automated deployment and audit-trail generation through AnthillPro and supporting tooling.
  • Defined release sign-off criteria and chaired go/no-go decision meetings.
  • Supported regulatory audits including FFIEC, PCI, and SOC-related reviews.

Outcome

  • Reduced deployment duration from approximately two hours to eight minutes.
  • Improved release predictability and operational consistency across multiple products.
  • Reduced manual effort while strengthening auditability and governance.
  • Enabled engineering teams to deliver more frequently with lower operational risk.

Lessons Learned

Sustainable delivery improvements come from combining automation, governance, and accountability. Faster releases only create value when teams can trust the process behind them.

Platform delivery

Intuit

Delivery recovery & environment stability

Context

Platform teams were missing release commitments while developers lost time to unstable non-production environments. Delivery credibility and engineering throughput were both at risk.

Challenge

Releases had slipped consistently, environment stability sat near 50%, and release bottlenecks were suppressing engineering productivity across dependent teams.

My Role

I led a recovery program spanning delivery governance, environment ownership, and capacity planning—partnering with engineering leaders to restore predictable execution.

Execution

  • Introduced stage-gate governance, program dashboards, and capacity planning.
  • Defined environment tiers, SLAs, and on-call ownership for development systems.
  • Removed release bottlenecks through sequencing, dependency management, and workflow changes.
  • Tracked delivery and stability metrics with weekly executive readouts.

Outcome

  • Restored on-time delivery for platforms that had 100% slipped releases.
  • Improved development environment stability from 50% to 98%.
  • Increased engineering productivity by approximately 20%.

Lessons Learned

When delivery and platform health fail together, recovery requires visible metrics, explicit ownership, and ruthless prioritization. Stabilizing the inner loop pays back quickly in team velocity.

Product delivery

Ealu.ai

Ealu.ai: Applying Product, Governance, and Delivery Principles to AI

Why I Built It

While completing my Executive MBA, I created Ealu.ai to explore the practical challenges of building AI-powered products.

The goal was not simply to experiment with large language models. I wanted to understand how product strategy, governance, compliance, usability, and delivery practices needed to evolve in an AI-driven environment.

Product Decisions

Many of the decisions made during development were informed by lessons learned across fintech, healthtech, and regulated technology organizations.

Security and Identity

Rather than treating security as a future enhancement, I required two-factor authentication from the initial release. In regulated environments, identity assurance is a product requirement rather than a technical afterthought.

Privacy by Design

The platform was designed to identify and suppress Social Security numbers contained within uploaded documents. This approach reflects the privacy-first mindset required when handling sensitive information and demonstrates how governance requirements can be incorporated into product design from day one.

Human-Centered AI

A core objective was improving usability rather than maximizing technical complexity. Product decisions focused on reducing friction, improving clarity, and helping users understand what the system was doing and why.

Delivery and Operations

The project provided an opportunity to apply release management, risk management, production readiness, and operational excellence practices within a modern AI application stack.

What I Learned

Building Ealu.ai reinforced a lesson that applies across both traditional software and AI systems: successful products require more than technology. Security, privacy, governance, usability, trust, and operational discipline must be designed into the product from the beginning.
