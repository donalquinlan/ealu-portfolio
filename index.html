Crisis program management · Intuit Payroll

Delivering the Paycheck Protection Program in 18 Days

Context

In 2020, the CARES Act created the Paycheck Protection Program (PPP) to provide emergency funding for small businesses during the COVID-19 pandemic.

For Intuit's payroll customers, speed was critical. Businesses needed immediate access to PPP-related capabilities while regulatory guidance continued to evolve.

Challenge

The team had approximately 18 days from legislation to delivery.

Requirements were changing rapidly, multiple stakeholders were involved, and the solution needed to support compliance across federal, state, and local jurisdictions.

My Role

As Senior Technical Program Manager for Intuit Payroll, I coordinated delivery efforts across engineering, product, operations, and tax teams.

I managed dependencies, tracked delivery risks, coordinated stakeholder communication, and helped drive execution under an extremely compressed timeline.

Execution

  • Coordinated cross-functional teams to align on scope and delivery priorities.
  • Worked with Tax Operations teams to understand jurisdiction-specific requirements.
  • Managed delivery risks and dependencies throughout implementation.
  • Supported rapid post-launch response, including six hotfix releases over a two-week period.

Outcome

  • Delivered PPP capabilities within 18 days of the legislation becoming law.
  • Helped enable more than $10B in loans.
  • Supported more than 100,000 small-business jobs during a period of significant economic uncertainty.

Lessons Learned

High-velocity programs succeed when communication, risk management, and decision-making mechanisms are established before they are needed. Under extreme time pressure, execution discipline becomes even more important than process.

Release engineering · Intuit

High-volume deployment modernization

Context

Intuit's high-volume transaction and payments systems required predictable, safe releases at scale. Manual deployment processes were consuming roughly two hours per release window and creating bottlenecks for multiple engineering teams.

Challenge

Release cycles were slow, inconsistently governed, and contributing to production risk. Multiple teams needed a shared release model without sacrificing compliance or rollback safety.

My Role

As Senior Technical Program Manager, I owned cross-team execution for release pipeline modernization—aligning engineering, operations, and leadership on scope, sequencing, and measurable outcomes.

Execution

  • Aligned six engineering teams on a consistent release model and governance checkpoints.
  • Introduced risk-based release governance tied to deployment safety and rollback readiness.
  • Built dashboards for release health, incident trends, and executive visibility.
  • Drove automation and process changes to shorten the critical path to production.

Outcome

  • Reduced deployment windows from ~2 hours to under 10 minutes.
  • Reduced production incidents by 15% across payments systems.
  • Improved predictability and stakeholder confidence in release cadence.

Lessons Learned

Release transformation succeeds when governance, tooling, and team accountability change together. Dashboards and clear ownership turn release health from a subjective debate into an executable program.

Platform delivery · Intuit

Delivery recovery & environment stability

Context

Platform teams were missing release commitments while developers lost time to unstable non-production environments. Delivery credibility and engineering throughput were both at risk.

Challenge

Releases had slipped consistently, environment stability sat near 50%, and release bottlenecks were suppressing engineering productivity across dependent teams.

My Role

I led a recovery program spanning delivery governance, environment ownership, and capacity planning—partnering with engineering leaders to restore predictable execution.

Execution

  • Introduced stage-gate governance, program dashboards, and capacity planning.
  • Defined environment tiers, SLAs, and on-call ownership for development systems.
  • Removed release bottlenecks through sequencing, dependency management, and workflow changes.
  • Tracked delivery and stability metrics with weekly executive readouts.

Outcome

  • Restored on-time delivery for platforms that had 100% slipped releases.
  • Improved development environment stability from 50% to 98%.
  • Increased engineering productivity by approximately 20%.

Lessons Learned

When delivery and platform health fail together, recovery requires visible metrics, explicit ownership, and ruthless prioritization. Stabilizing the inner loop pays back quickly in team velocity.

Ealu.ai · Product delivery

Intake Flow — patient intake with AI-assisted autofill

Context

Patient intake often repeats the same medical history across forms and formats. The goal was a practical product that reduces manual rework while staying credible for real clinical workflows.

Challenge

Build a secure, end-to-end intake experience supporting PDF, Word, and image uploads—with AI-assisted field detection, review before finalize, and a hosted demo suitable for stakeholders and users.

My Role

As Founder and Technical Program Manager at Ealu.ai, I owned product definition, delivery sequencing, and launch of Intake Flow from concept through production deployment.

Execution

  • Built review/edit workflow and downloadable completed PDF and Word outputs.
  • Deployed to Fly.io with CI/CD from GitHub Actions and a public demo environment.

Outcome
